Dr. Michael Brown

Michael S. Brown, born on April 13, 1941, in New York, is a Nobel laureate who received the Nobel Prize in Physiology or Medicine in 1985. He was affiliated with the University of Texas Southwestern Medical Center at Dallas at the time of the award. Brown, along with Joseph Goldstein, discovered the receptor in cells that takes in cholesterol and clarified how cholesterol conversion is regulated by genes and other substances. Their discoveries laid the foundation for statins, medications that reduce cholesterol levels in the blood
